webpack

Webpack is a static asset and JavaScript bundler used by millions of developers. With over 33,000 stars on github and 4,000+ forks, it is arguably the most popular JavaScript bundler used today around the world.

I am one of the newest members of the Webpack core development team helping triage issues, organize future development plans, and oversee both webpack-dev-middleware and webpack-dev-server as lead as of June 2017. Analytics and Tracking

At Gilt.com I was able to redefine client event tracking and analytics collection processes, which provided far greater data fidelity and reliability for both in-house and third party services. Development of the Gumshoe analytics and event library was key to this effort and allowed us to validate, in real-time, eventing and event data. The initiative led to a 40% increase in the volume of data collected, 30% rise in partner revenue, and translated to a 15% savings due to increased pricing and purchasing accuracy.

I lead the project to audit and redevelopment the tagging and tracking platforms for both Gilt.com and Saks.com. The effort resulted in a 50% reduction in total load time for Gilt.com and an 85% reduction for Saks.com.

Node.js

When I joined Gilt.com their stack was anchored by a distributed Scala platform. This was cumbersome and presented a high learning curve to Front End developers at the company. Deployments of the Scala stack were measured in hours. I was tasked with and lead the project to develop a Node.js Framework for web applications, upon which all of Gilt.com's individual web apps would run.

Since implementation of the Node Framework, deployment time for apps fell by 98%, development of new features and maintenance of existing code require one quarter of the time required prior, for a sizable savings in developer-hours.
